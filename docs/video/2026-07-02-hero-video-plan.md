# Hero Video — Content Plan v2

**Concept: «Раньше / Теперь» (Before / Now)**
2026-07-02 · v2 — generalized after founder feedback («barber as the spine feels
limiting»), restructured around PwC's 2026 "So you can" campaign film (30s).
Locked decisions: real footage + VFX, **no one fronting camera**; **Russian
voiceover** + per-locale subtitles; **~30s** single cut (down from 45–60s —
PwC proves 30s carries a full arc, and it halves the file size).

---

## 1. The one-sentence idea

Universal moments of business chaos — a ringing phone nobody answers, a paper
ledger, a wall of sticky notes — flip into the same frames running calmly on
invisible systems, with the "magic" layer rendered as **brand-red (#E5484D)
data/UI overlays on real footage**, and every big number withheld so the stats
section below delivers the punch.

## 2. What we took from the PwC film (verified from their own assets)

Their campaign-film caption file is public — this is the full 30s script:

> *Before, data was just information. Now it's the start of everything. / We
> optimise the systems that put it to work. / Scale your AI from proof of
> concept to competitive edge. / And accelerate your tech evolution so you're
> redefining what's possible. / From blueprint to breakthrough, we take you
> further with tech / so you can outthink, outpace, and outperform. /
> PwC. So you can.*

What makes it work, and what we adopt:

1. **"Before → Now" reframe in one line** — not a long chaos story. Opens the
   arc instantly. We adopt this as the spine.
2. **Parallel capability beats** — short verb-led lines in the same rhythm.
   No client anecdotes, no numbers. Category-level, so every viewer projects
   their own business onto it. *This is the "general" quality the founder wants.*
3. **Escalating triad** before the sign-off ("outthink, outpace, outperform").
4. **Brand sign-off that completes the campaign line** ("PwC. So you can.").
5. **Architecture**: their muted hero loop is a separate ambient asset (file
   literally named `loop-home-aigenerated.mp4` — it's AI-generated footage);
   the film with voice lives behind a click **with VTT captions** — exactly
   the muted-loop + unmute + VTT structure we already planned.
6. **Look** (from their poster frame): bright, natural, real people — with
   glowing brand-colored data graphics composited onto real surfaces (a glass
   table becomes a dashboard). Our version: **#E5484D overlays on documentary
   footage**. The VFX layer *is* the brand color.

What we deliberately do differently: PwC can speak in pure abstraction because
the logo carries the proof. Freshman cannot — so one beat of *real running
systems* stays in the film (screens only, quick), and the founder-at-work beat
stays for local trust. Work first, face second (post-2024 CIS anti-guru
findings, unchanged from v1 research).

## 3. Research foundations that still govern v2 (adversarially verified)

- ~69–85% will only ever watch muted → the film must read with zero audio.
- First 3 seconds carry up to 47% of brand value → open mid-action, no logo.
- Mobile object-cover shows only the central ~26–33% of the frame → center-punch everything; check on the iPhone 9:16 grid.
- Telegram UI on screen is a native trust cue for a ~76%-Telegram audience.
- Zero burned-in words (3 locales); subtitles ship as `hero.{ru,en,uz}.vtt`.
- No editorial statistics in the video — the stakes section below is the payoff.
- Banned imagery: slow-mo handshakes, skylines, stock typing, glowing AI brains, code rain, robots, buzzword cards.

## 5. Shot list (kit-matched, generalized)

Everything locked tripod, 4K/25fps, **1/50 shutter** (50Hz mains — no flicker),
manual exposure/WB. Every setup: main take → clean plate → 2–3s handles.
**Grade: natural and bright** (PwC direction) — the site's CSS scrim darkens the
text zones, so footage does not need to be moody. VFX carries the red.

**Vignette set A — "the counter" (~1.5h).** Any real counter works: café,
retail, or Bilol's shop *framed neutral* (no barber chair in the hero framing —
it's "a business", not "a barbershop"). Shots: phone hero (hook + calm mirror +
loop-seam order), paper ledger close-up, busy-hands bokeh.

**Vignette set B — "the paperwork desk" (~1.5h).** Home/office staged: document
mountain, sticky-note wall (one falls — practical, no VFX needed), spreadsheet +
chat queue on screen (real UI + blank clean plate), thin-folder mirror shot,
8–10 stop-motion stills of the shrinking stack.

**Set C — founder desk on backdrop (~2h).** COB+softbox key 45° slightly above
eye level, wall-bounce fill, **RGB light set to #E5484D as rim** — the brand
accent exists in-camera. Founder-at-work medium, the look-up resolve (several
takes, calm), over-the-shoulder screens running the real systems + clean plates.

**Screen recordings (no camera):** booking bot full flow (iPhone QuickTime
mirror), grading flow, delivered raw for post.

## 6. Delivery specs

- Master 4K/25 → deliver **1080p H.264** (CRF 23–24, `yuv420p`,
  `-movflags +faststart`), AAC audio kept. 30s ≈ **5–7MB**. Optional AV1/WebM.
- **Poster frame**: iOS Low Power Mode shows only the poster — use the calm
  counter or founder frame with red VFX visible; ≤200KB; verify all three
  headlines at 4.5:1 on it.
- Subtitles: `hero.ru.vtt`, `hero.en.vtt`, `hero.uz.vtt` (PwC ships VTT for
  their film too — same pattern).
- Drop-in: overwrite `public/hero.mp4` + `public/hero-poster.jpg`.

**Site TODOs when footage lands:** `<track>` captions per locale · pause
control (WCAG 2.2.2) · `preload="none"` + poster · 9:16 re-cut for
Telegram/Instagram from the 4K master.

## 7. Music / SFX

Minimal and warm (unmute layer only): room tone, soft ticks as red UI resolves,
one low pad at the turn. Royalty-free license (Artlist/Epidemic/Uppbeat).
Nothing epic, nothing trending.

## 9. Decision log

- **v1 (barber-spine "Same Counter, Two Days") superseded** — founder: too
  limiting. The before/after match-cut DNA survives; the industry-specific
  story does not. Real cases demoted from spine to one proof beat.
- **50s → 30s** — PwC's film carries a complete arc in 30s; shorter also lands
  the file at ~5–7MB, resolving the v1 size tension.
- **Dark grade → natural/bright + red VFX** — matches the PwC look and the new
  site accent (#E5484D); the CSS scrim already protects text legibility.
- Still rejected, unchanged: talking-head pitch (muted lips read broken; guru-
  adjacent), burned-in captions (3 locales), stats in the video (stakes section
  is the payoff), 8–15s ambient-only loop (founder chose a story).
